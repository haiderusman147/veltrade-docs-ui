In crypto, security is not an advanced topic. It is the starting point. Most losses do not come from bad trades. They come from simple mistakes: clicking a phishing link, exposing a recovery phrase, or relying on weak account protection.

Security is not about knowing more. It is about doing a few things consistently.

## The non-negotiables

### Strong authentication

Your first layer of protection is account access.

- Enable **two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all accounts
- Prefer authenticator apps over SMS when possible
- Use long, unique passwords for each platform

If a password is reused or easily guessable, the rest of your setup becomes irrelevant.

### Verify links and applications

Phishing is one of the most common causes of loss.

- Type important URLs manually instead of clicking links
- Bookmark official websites and use those bookmarks
- Download wallet apps only from verified sources

Many attacks look identical to legitimate platforms. The difference is often only in the URL.

### Secure your email

Your email account is often used for password resets and account recovery.

- protect it with a strong password and 2FA
- avoid using it casually across unrelated services
- treat it as part of your financial security, not just communication

If your email is compromised, other protections can be bypassed.

## Wallet safety (where mistakes become irreversible)

### Recovery phrase handling

The recovery phrase (seed phrase) is the ultimate control point.

- store it offline in a secure location
- never save it in screenshots, notes, or cloud storage
- never share it, even if a site or person appears legitimate

If someone has access to your recovery phrase, they do not need your password. They control the funds.

### Separate usage by purpose

A simple structure reduces risk:

- use a hot wallet for small amounts and active use
- store long-term holdings in cold storage

This limits the impact of mistakes or compromises in everyday activity.

## Exchange safety habits

Exchanges are convenient but introduce dependency on the platform.

1. Enable withdrawal confirmations and anti-phishing codes if available
2. Use withdrawal address whitelisting where supported
3. Test deposits and withdrawals with small amounts first
4. Avoid storing large balances on exchanges longer than necessary

The goal is not to eliminate exchange use, but to reduce exposure.

## Device and environment awareness

Security is not only about accounts. It also depends on the device you use.

- keep your operating system and apps updated
- avoid installing unknown extensions or software
- be cautious when using public or shared networks

A compromised device can bypass otherwise strong account protections.

## The mindset that prevents most losses

Most security failures share a common pattern: urgency.

- messages that create pressure (“account issue”, “claim now”)
- links that appear official but are slightly altered
- requests that bypass normal verification steps

A simple rule helps: **slow down before acting**.

If something feels urgent, verify it independently before proceeding.

## Summary

Most crypto-related losses come from predictable and preventable mistakes: weak authentication, phishing attacks, poor recovery phrase handling, and careless device usage.

A small set of consistent habits provides strong protection. In practice, disciplined security behavior protects more capital than any advanced trading strategy.
