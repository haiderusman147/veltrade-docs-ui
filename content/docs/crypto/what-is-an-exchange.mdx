A cryptocurrency exchange is a platform that allows users to buy, sell, and trade digital assets. Exchanges serve as the primary gateway between individuals and the cryptocurrency market, providing infrastructure for order matching, liquidity, and price discovery.

## Centralized exchanges

Centralized exchanges (CEX) are operated by companies that manage user accounts and custody assets.

Common features include:

- user accounts and identity verification
- order books matching buyers and sellers
- integrated wallets for deposits and withdrawals

Examples of services offered by centralized exchanges include spot trading, margin trading, and futures contracts.

Centralized exchanges often provide a user-friendly interface, making them popular among beginners.

## Decentralized exchanges

Decentralized exchanges (DEX) operate differently. Instead of relying on a central operator, DEX platforms use smart contracts to execute trades directly on a blockchain.

Characteristics of decentralized exchanges include:

- non-custodial trading
- interaction through crypto wallets
- liquidity pools rather than traditional order books

DEX platforms often provide greater transparency but may require more technical understanding.

## Liquidity and order books

Liquidity refers to the availability of buyers and sellers at various price levels. Exchanges maintain order books where traders submit buy and sell orders.

High liquidity generally leads to:

- tighter spreads
- faster execution
- more stable prices

Low liquidity can result in greater volatility and larger price swings during trades.

## Exchange fees

Most exchanges generate revenue through trading fees. These fees typically vary depending on:

- trading volume
- order type (maker vs taker)
- platform policies

Understanding fee structures helps traders avoid unnecessary costs.

## Security considerations

Because exchanges hold user assets, security is an important factor when selecting a platform. Important considerations include:

- account protection measures such as two-factor authentication
- cold storage of funds
- withdrawal security procedures

Users should also remain cautious of phishing attempts and fake applications.

## Summary

Cryptocurrency exchanges serve as the primary marketplace for digital asset trading. Whether centralized or decentralized, these platforms connect buyers and sellers while facilitating price discovery. Understanding how exchanges operate helps traders interact with markets more safely and efficiently.
