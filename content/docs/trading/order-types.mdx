In financial markets, trades are executed through orders submitted to an exchange or broker. Different order types determine how and when a trade is executed. Understanding these mechanisms helps traders control execution quality and reduce unnecessary costs.

## Market orders

A **market order** instructs the exchange to execute a trade immediately at the best available price.

Market orders are commonly used when execution speed is more important than price precision.

Advantages:

- instant execution
- simplicity

Disadvantages:

- price may differ slightly from expectations during volatile conditions

In fast-moving markets, market orders can produce slippage, where the final execution price differs from the displayed price.

## Limit orders

A **limit order** allows traders to specify the maximum price they are willing to pay (for buying) or the minimum price they will accept (for selling).

Advantages:

- price control
- reduced slippage

Disadvantages:

- the order may not fill if the market never reaches the specified price

Limit orders are often used when entering trades near predefined support or resistance levels.

## Stop orders

A **stop order** activates once the market reaches a predefined trigger price.

Stop orders are typically used for:

- stop-loss protection
- breakout trading strategies

For example, a trader may place a stop order above a resistance level to enter the market if price breaks through that level.

## Stop-limit orders

A stop-limit order combines features of stop and limit orders. Once the stop price is triggered, the order becomes a limit order rather than a market order.

This provides price control but introduces the risk that the order may not execute if the market moves too quickly.

## Choosing the right order type

The appropriate order type depends on the trading situation.

Market orders are useful when:

- entering highly liquid markets
- execution speed is critical

Limit orders are useful when:

- precise entry prices are required
- markets are stable

Stop orders are useful when:

- protecting against losses
- entering momentum-based trades

Understanding these differences helps traders execute strategies more efficiently.

## Summary

Order types determine how trades interact with the market. By choosing the appropriate order structure, traders can control execution speed, price precision, and risk exposure. Even simple strategies benefit from careful order selection.
